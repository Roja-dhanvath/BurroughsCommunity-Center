Who we are

OUR MISSION is to provide the residents of Bridgeport with program opportunities and facility resources for the purpose of promoting individual growth and community cohesiveness.

We fulfill this through our direct services and by collaborating with community and facility partners. We also do this by connecting people requesting information, programs or forms of aid with agencies in the Greater Bridgeport community.

The Burroughs Community Center offers a variety of programs including the arts, fitness, financial literacy as well as meeting space for clubs.
The Burroughs Community Center is also a Nonprofit Center that houses several not-for-profit organizations. Each organization has its own office space and access to meeting rooms, a board room, a fully equipped kitchen and a large parking lot. Located in the Black Rock section of the West End of Bridgeport, this beautiful historic building is a welcoming presence for clients and visitors of the organizations who are our “ facility partners. ”

If you or your organization is a not-for-profit and interested in becoming a facility partner please contact the Executive Director at 203-334-0293 or info@burroughscc.org

Our History

“The Burroughs”, located on Fairfield Avenue in Black Rock, has been a cornerstone of the Black Rock and West End Bridgeport communities for over a century. It was originally a home for single working women. In 1995, when there was little demand for this type of housing, the property was put up for sale. A group of area residents raised the funds to purchase and renovate the beautiful historic building to create a community center.

Before opening the community was surveyed and found that the greatest need was for after-school programs for middle school children. This age group was at the highest risk as there were few options for them after school. In addition, the newly established Burroughs Community Center began to offer adult programming and soon became home to many non-profit groups that serve the Bridgeport area.

In June 2011 the youth programs moved next door to the new Smilow-Burroughs Clubhouse. After surveying residents of the surrounding neighborhood, the Burroughs Community Center introduced new, adult-focused programs. The “new” Burroughs now offers programming for older youth and adults, with a special interest in serving the surrounding neighborhood.
