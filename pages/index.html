Who we are

At Burroughs, we believe the strength of a community is measured by the shared understanding of its strengths, weaknesses and commitment to building partnerships. A healthy community will celebrate diversity, act inclusively and work intentionally to reduce barriers to success. Burroughs will use its facility resources and leadership role to foster community success. We will support partnerships that overcome barriers to opportunity by advancing knowledge and foster understanding.

Reserve your room

Burroughs has a variety of rooms available for organization to use to host trainings, meetings or program activities. With flexible room set ups, Burroughs can accommodate groups of between 2 and 80 people depending on set-up and configuration. To help ensure your success, Burroughs is often able to support your activity by making available flip chart easels, LCD projectors, Wi-Fi and computers. The Burroughs Community Center does not host private social functions.

Our partners

  • The Bridgeport Child Advocacy Coalition is a coalition of organizations, parents, and other concerned individuals committed to improving the well-being of Bridgeport's children through research, education, advocacy, and mobilization.

  • Big Brothers Big Sisters of Southwestern Connecticut is a non-profit agency that provides adult mentors to children. They recruit, carefully screen and train caring adults to act as mentors, match them with children, and support them.

  • RYASAP (Regional Youth Adult Social Action Partnership) works to ensure the safe and healthy development of youth, young adults and families by actively engaging organizations, public officials and community leaders around issues that matter most to the community.

  • The Witness Project of CT seeks to reduce the number of low income, minority women diagnosed with late stage breast cancer in the Greater Fairfield County.

  • BAYSO

    As part of the American Youth Soccer Organization, their mission is to provide a rewarding soccer experience for the children, parents, and volunteers in the communities that they serve. The Region is a part of AYSO Area 3B (New England States), and of AYSO Section 3.

  • Black Rock Community Council

    Founded in 1976, the Black Rock Community Council (BRCC) is the oldest and largest community membership organization in the area.
