Programs

With an emphasis on reducing barriers to success, Burroughs programs offer individuals the opportunity to obtain instruction and connect with services they may not otherwise receive. Our programs recognize the importance of remaining flexibility to meet the changing needs of our community while staying focused on delivering outcomes most meaning full to the participants. For more information, please, contact us.

Contact us!

The Quick Start Language program is a self-driven computer assisted language instruction. Computers are made available to participants along with the software instruction in English and 10 other languages. Someone is available on site for support. Contact us if you are interested in learning a new language.

The Volunteer Income Tax Assistance program offers free tax help to people who generally make $54,000 or less, persons with disabilities, the elderly and limited English speaking taxpayers who need assistance in preparing their own tax returns. IRS-certified volunteers provide free basic income tax return preparation with electronic filing to qualified individuals.

Burroughs partners with the Literacy Volunteers of Greater Bridgeport to actively promote literacy by identifying adult literacy needs in our community and meeting those needs through direct literacy services. We strive to help our clients acquire the literacy skills necessary to be successful in the workplace, participate in the education of their children and lead productive lives.

Our Computer Center is equipped with computers with access to the standard Microsoft Office Suite of programs and access to the internet. Burroughs also offers personalized computer support that allows the participant to define their own learning objectives. This allows students to focus on the competencies they need to develop at a pace they feel comfortable. Contact us if access to a computer or basic knowledge of how to use a computer is an obstacle for you.

The Our Woven Community (OWC) program provides refugee women the opportunity to become self-sufficient leaders, integrate into the community and become empowered with entrepreneurial skills and economic opportunity. Learning to use sewing machines, they items that are sold at local craft fairs and holiday events. Half of the proceeds from each item sold goes to the woman who created it, the rest helps to keep the program running.
